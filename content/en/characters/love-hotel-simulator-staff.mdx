<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Staff Integration">

Integrating new **love hotel simulator staff** into your workflow requires a systematic approach. You cannot simply hire someone and expect them to know exactly what to do. You must assign them to specific zones and set their schedules to ensure 24/7 coverage.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Zone Assignment">
    Open the management menu and designate which rooms each staff member is responsible for. For beginners, assign one Maid to every three rooms to ensure maximum cleanliness.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Schedule Optimization">
    Set up shifts to cover peak hours. Most guests arrive in the evening, so ensure your Receptionist is on duty from 6:00 PM to 2:00 AM.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Equipment Provision">
    Purchase higher-tier cleaning supplies and tools. Better vacuums and repair kits significantly reduce the time staff spend on individual tasks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Performance Review">

</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Handling Staff Issues and Retention">

Employees in the simulator are not robots. They have needs, moods, and sometimes they will cause trouble. Managing the human element of your **love hotel simulator staff** is what separates a successful hotelier from a failing one. If a staff member is unhappy, they may start "skimming" from the register or ignoring trash in the rooms.

| Issue | Potential Cause | Best Solution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Low Morale** | Low pay or poor break room quality | Increase salary or upgrade lounge |
| **High Turnover** | Overworked staff / no breaks | Hire more staff or adjust shifts |
| **Frequent Errors** | Low intelligence or lack of training | Invest in training modules |
| **Theft** | Extremely low happiness or bad personality | Immediate termination / Security cams |

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Firing a staff member with a high "Social" stat can lead to other employees becoming upset. Always try to resolve issues through bonuses or improved conditions before resorting to termination.
</Callout>
