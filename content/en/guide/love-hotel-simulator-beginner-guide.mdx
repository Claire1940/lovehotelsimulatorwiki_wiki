<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Room Layout: Designing High-Yield Spaces">

Building a successful business requires a deep understanding of room layouts and guest preferences. In the early stages of the game, space is your most valuable asset. Efficiently placing furniture ensures that guests have access to all necessary amenities without causing pathfinding issues. A standard room must contain a bed, lighting, and a bathroom area, but adding theme-specific decorations will greatly increase the room value.

When designing your first few rooms, try to leave at least a one-tile gap around the bed to allow staff to clean the area quickly. Rooms that are cluttered or difficult to navigate will slow down your cleaning staff, leading to longer wait times for waiting guests.

<Section icon={Target} title="love hotel simulator beginner guide: Financial Strategies">

Managing your finances is the most critical aspect of keeping your business operational. Many new players fail because they spend too much money on expensive decorations before securing a stable daily income. Your primary focus during the first week should be maintaining positive cash flow. Keep track of your daily utility bills and staff wages, as these recurring costs can quickly drain your bank account if your occupancy rate drops.

Adjusting your room rates is a powerful tool to control guest flow. If you notice long lines at the reception desk, it is a clear sign that your prices are too low. Conversely, if your rooms sit empty for hours, consider lowering the rates temporarily to attract budget-conscious customers.

| Upgrade Item | Initial Cost | Energy Consumption | Profit Boost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Neon Lighting** | Cheap | **Low** | +10% |
| **Jacuzzi Tub** | Expensive | **High** | +45% |
| **Soundproof Walls** | Moderate | **None** | +25% |

Monitoring your utility usage is also essential. High-end appliances like jacuzzis and advanced entertainment systems consume significant amounts of electricity. Turn off appliances in unoccupied rooms to keep utility bills manageable.

<Callout type="warning" title="Financial Warning">
Avoid taking out maximum loans in the early game. The high interest rates can easily overwhelm your daily revenue, leading to bankruptcy before you can establish a stable customer base.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Staffing Guide: Hiring and Training Employees">

An efficient workforce is the backbone of your hotel operations. While you can handle check-ins and cleaning yourself in the beginning, you will quickly become overwhelmed as you build more rooms. Hiring staff allows you to automate daily tasks and focus on expanding your business. Each employee type has specific duties, and balancing their workloads is key to maintaining high service quality.

Cleanliness is the most important factor in guest reviews. If a room is dirty, guests will leave immediately and post negative feedback online, which lowers your overall reputation. Therefore, hiring a reliable cleaner should be your very first personnel decision.

| Staff Role | Monthly Salary | Key Responsibility | Efficiency Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cleaner** | **$800** | Room Sanitization | High |
| **Receptionist** | **$1,200** | Guest Check-in | Medium |
| **Security** | **$1,500** | Preventing Incidents | High |

As your revenue grows, invest in training your staff. Trained employees work faster, consume less energy, and handle customer complaints more effectively, which directly translates to higher ratings and profits.

<Callout type="success" title="Management Success">
Prioritize training your cleaning staff first. Faster cleaning times mean rooms are prepared for the next guests quicker, directly boosting your maximum daily revenue.
</Callout>
