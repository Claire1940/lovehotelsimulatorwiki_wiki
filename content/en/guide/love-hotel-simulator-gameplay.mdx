<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Renovation Phase: Cleaning and Trash Removal">

The first hurdle in **love hotel simulator gameplay** is the sheer volume of filth. Players often find that their "hands just give up" when faced with the warehouse-like piles of trash. This isn't just a cosmetic step; the game tracks "stench levels" that directly impact your ability to attract staff or customers. You must systematically work through each room, removing debris and scrubbing surfaces to prepare for furniture assembly.

| Trash Type | Removal Method | Impact on Appeal |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Industrial Debris** | Manual Disposal | High Improvement |
| **Surface Stains** | Scrubbing/Cleaning | Medium Improvement |
| **Odor Sources** | Deep Cleaning | Essential for Staffing |
| **Old Scrap** | Recycling/Selling | Minor Revenue Gain |

  <Card title="Budget Management">

<Section icon={Target} title="Furniture Assembly and Room Configuration">

Once the space is clean, the true **love hotel simulator gameplay** loop moves into interior design. Furniture arrives in boxes and must be manually assembled and placed. This isn't just about aesthetics; the placement of beds, lighting, and decorative items determines the "Love Level" of the room. You can even give your rooms custom names to build a brand, though the game suggests keeping them short and catchy for the "ledger."

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Unpack Furniture Boxes">
    Locate the delivery area and move boxes into the designated rooms. Ensure you have the full set (bed, nightstands, lighting) before starting.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Manual Assembly">
    Interact with the boxes to assemble the furniture. While the furniture might not be the "newest" at the start, its arrangement is what matters for early clients.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Thematic Naming">
    Use the room interface to name your suites. Creative names can help you track which rooms are performing best in your business ledger.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Final Inspection">
    Walk through the room to ensure there is enough space for NPCs to navigate. Cramped rooms can lead to negative feedback.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| **Basic (Starter)** | Low | Low | 15% |
| **Standard** | Medium | Medium | 40% |

<Section icon={Shield} title="Staff Recruitment and Neighborhood Scouting">

A love hotel cannot run on a single owner alone. A significant part of the **love hotel simulator gameplay** involves walking around the neighborhood to find and convince people to work for you. This social simulation aspect requires you to build a reputation first. If your hotel looks like a barn or smells like a factory, potential employees like Camila or other neighborhood residents will be hesitant to join your team.
