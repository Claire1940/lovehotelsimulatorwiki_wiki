<Section icon={Target} title="High-Yield Layout Strategies for 2026">

Optimizing your layout is about more than just placing items where they fit. You must consider the pathing of both guests and staff. If a room is too cluttered, cleaning staff will take longer to reset it, leading to lost revenue during peak hours. Conversely, a room that is too empty will feel "cheap" and result in lower ratings.

The most successful layouts in the 2026 meta involve a "Zone" approach. Divide your room into three distinct zones: the Entry Zone (storage and mirrors), the Entertainment Zone (TV and seating), and the Relaxation Zone (the bed and mood lighting).

| Layout Strategy | Pros | Cons | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Open Concept** | Fast cleaning, high flow | Harder to hit prestige caps | Small rooms |
| **Zoned Design** | High satisfaction, clear vibe | Requires more space/budget | Deluxe suites |
| **Max Density** | Highest possible prestige | High maintenance, slow cleaning | VIP farming |
| **Minimalist** | Lowest cost, easy to manage | Low profit per guest | Early game |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Clear the Canvas">
    Start with an empty room and select a wall color or wallpaper that sets the foundation for your theme. Darker colors work best for neon-heavy designs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Anchor the Bed">
    The bed is the centerpiece. Place it in a position that allows for 360-degree movement if possible, or against a feature wall for maximum visual impact.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Add Essential Amenities">
    Install the bathroom fixtures and basic storage. Guests will complain immediately if these are missing, regardless of how good the rest of the room looks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Layer the Lighting">
    Add your primary ceiling lights first, then layer in neon strips and floor lamps to create depth and highlight specific furniture pieces.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Final Decorations">
    Fill small gaps with rugs, plants, or wall art. Check the room's "Prestige Meter" to ensure you've reached the target threshold for that room tier.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Clutter">
While it is tempting to fill every square inch with decor, the "Clutter Penalty" is real. If the room density exceeds 85%, guests may report feeling "cramped," which caps their maximum satisfaction level.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Settings} title="Advanced Design Troubleshooting">

Even the best designers encounter issues. Guests might find a room "unbalanced" or complain about noise if the room is too close to the lobby or elevator. Monitoring guest feedback is the only way to fine-tune your designs for maximum efficiency.

Common issues often stem from a lack of "Visual Balance." If all your expensive furniture is on one side of the room, the other side will feel neglected. Use rugs and lighting to bridge the gap between different furniture groups.

| Issue | Potential Cause | Recommended Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Low Comfort Score** | Cheap bedding or no seating | Upgrade to memory foam or add a sofa |
| **Slow Cleaning** | Too many small objects on floor | Swap floor decor for wall-mounted items |
| **Negative Mood** | Poor lighting or clashing colors | Use a consistent color palette and dimmers |
| **Cramped Rating** | Poor furniture spacing | Remove 1-2 non-essential items |

<Callout type="info" title="The 60-30-10 Rule">
For the best visual results, use a primary color for 60% of the room (walls/floors), a secondary color for 30% (furniture), and an accent color for 10% (neons/decor).
</Callout>
